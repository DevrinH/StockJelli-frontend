Weekly Crypto Report — Apr 25, 2026

Apr 25, 2026 · 4 min read

Here's a look at the crypto assets that showed the strongest momentum on StockJelli's screener during the week of April 19–25. This report recaps what moved, the volume behind it, and how push notification alerts performed. All data is pulled directly from StockJelli's screener entries throughout the week.

Week Overview

Bitcoin ranged from -2.48% to +4.60% intraday across the week. Total crypto market moved between -2.30% and +3.52%. StockJelli's crypto screener logged 429 entries across 207 unique tokens — a very active week with Tuesday (April 22) being the busiest day at 77 entries. The weekend was also notably active, with 28 entries on Saturday and 39 on Sunday.

Top Screener Entries This Week

Ranked by peak percentage gain from the screener entry price.

1. APE (ApeCoin) — peaked at +151.2% from entry

ApeCoin entered the screener on Friday with a modest 5.3% entry move, but the volume-to-market-cap ratio was just 0.15 at that point. The token then exploded — volume surged to over $1B against a ~$199M market cap on a later entry, pushing the V/MC ratio above 5.0. This was the week's single largest mover and a textbook example of a slow entry that accelerated into a major breakout.

2. OPG (OpenGradient) — peaked at +147.3% from entry

Entered the screener on Saturday at +3.0% from its 24-hour open with a small-cap market cap of $53M. Low volume-to-market-cap ratio (0.02) at entry, but the token steadily built momentum throughout the weekend.

3. CHIP — peaked at +116.2% from entry

Entered Monday at +5.7% with a mid-cap market cap of $129M and strong volume backing at V/MC of 3.73. The heavy volume relative to market cap at entry signaled conviction behind the move, and the token more than doubled from its screener entry price.

Push Notification Performance

The crypto notification channels fired 19 alerts this week across CH1 (fast movers) and CH3 (slow burners). Of those, 11 hit the +3% target from the alert price — a 58% hit rate for the week. 18 of 19 alerts (95%) stayed positive from the push price.

The standout alert was CORE, which gained +42.9% from its push price. Other strong performers included AXS (+35.9%), PIEVERSE (+31.7%), ENJ (+28.0%), and ORCA (+19.2%). The CH3 slow-burner channel contributed 5 of the 19 alerts, with IRYS (+8.9%) and ASTEROID (+5.5%) both hitting the target through the confirmation window.

Market Cap Breakdown

Of the 429 crypto screener entries this week:

  • Large cap ($1B+): 23 entries (5.4%) — primarily layer-1 and established DeFi tokens moving with the broader market
  • Mid cap ($100M–$1B): 192 entries (44.8%) — the most active segment by far
  • Small cap ($50M–$100M): 214 entries (49.9%) — highest entry count and home to the largest percentage moves

Small and mid caps together accounted for 94.6% of all screener entries. The week's top performers by peak percentage (APE, OPG, CHIP, SPK, BSB) were all in the $50M–$200M range at entry — consistent with capital flowing into higher-beta altcoins during a BTC-positive environment.

Volume Observations

Average 24-hour volume at entry was $61M. Several tokens showed extreme volume-to-market-cap ratios: KAT hit 12.61 V/MC, APE reached 5.48, and API3 came in at 4.35. Tokens with V/MC ratios above 1.0 generally produced the largest initial percentage moves but also showed faster mean reversion. The mid-range V/MC entries (0.3–0.8) tended to sustain their momentum longer.

BTC Correlation

BTC's intraday range of -2.48% to +4.60% created shifting conditions throughout the week. Tuesday was the clear hotspot with 77 entries — coinciding with one of BTC's stronger intraday runs. The weekend showed 67 entries combined (Saturday 28, Sunday 39), demonstrating that crypto momentum doesn't sleep. Activity was relatively even across the rest of the week, suggesting broad underlying participation rather than a single catalyst-driven surge.
