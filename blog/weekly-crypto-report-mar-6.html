What this tells us: Crypto entries performed very differently from stocks this week. The short-term windows (30 min, 1 hour) were slightly negative on average, with win rates of just 37–41%. Entries needed 4 hours to reach near-breakeven on win rate (49%), and even by end of day, the median entry was still down -0.75%.

However, the reward-to-risk asymmetry is still present: even at end of day with a 42% win rate, average wins (+5.16%) were significantly larger than average losses (-3.07%). A few big winners pulled the average gain positive (+0.38%) even though most individual entries were losers. This is classic momentum distribution — a small percentage of entries produce outsized gains that compensate for the majority that fade.

The takeaway: Crypto screener entries this week needed more patience than stocks. Quick 30-minute trades were a losing strategy on average. Traders who held through the initial noise and gave entries 4+ hours to develop had a much better chance. This is a critical insight for how we'll refine the SJ Score and alert thresholds going forward — the crypto formula may need a higher entry bar than stocks to filter for entries that sustain.

Note: This is one week of data (288 entries). These patterns may not persist across different market regimes. We're publishing for full transparency.
